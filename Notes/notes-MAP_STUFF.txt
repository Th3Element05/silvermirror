MAP DESIGN NOTES

It so happens that a 30x30 map will neatly fit into this limit, since (30+6)×(30+6)=1296.
However, keep in mind that the official maps are usually designed with the 
width as a multiple of 10 and the height as a multiple of 9. 
For example, small towns are often 10x9; large cities are 20x18; 
routes may be 20x9, or 10x18, or 10x45, or so on; 
National Park is 20x27; et cetera.
